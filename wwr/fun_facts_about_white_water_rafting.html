Fun Facts About White Water Rafting

White water rafting originated in the 1940s and gained popularity as an adventurous outdoor activity.

ivers are classified from Class I (easy) to Class VI (extremely difficult and dangerous) based on the level of difficulty for rafting.

The International Scale of River Difficulty is a standardized system used worldwide to classify rivers for rafting.

Some of the world's most famous white water rafting rivers include the Colorado River in the Grand Canyon, the Zambezi River in Africa, and the Futaleufu River in Chile.
